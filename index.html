A.P.J Abdul kalam

1931-2013

Missle man of india

Avul paki jainulabdeen abdul kalam A.P.J. Abdul Kalam was popularly known as the "Missile Man of India" because of his outstanding contributions to India's missile and defense technology. As a scientist at the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO), he played a key role in developing indigenous ballistic missiles such as Agni and Prithvi. He was also the Project Director of India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III), which successfully launched the Rohini satellite into orbit in 1980. His leadership in the Integrated Guided Missile Development Programme (IGMDP) strengthened India's defense capabilities and made the country more self-reliant in missile technology.
